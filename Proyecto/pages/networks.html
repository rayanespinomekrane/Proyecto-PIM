What Are Computer Networks?

Computer networks are the backbone of modern communication. Just like railroads connected towns in the old west, networks connect computers, servers, smartphones, and millions of devices around the world.

Networks allow users to share information, access online services, communicate instantly, and manage large systems efficiently. Without networking, the internet and modern computing would not exist.

This project explores the fundamentals of networking, including IP addressing, routers, switches, protocols, and network topologies.

02

NETWORK DEVICES

Modern networks rely on specialized hardware devices to transmit and manage information between systems.

Routers are responsible for directing traffic between different networks, while switches connect devices within the same local network. Access points provide wireless connectivity, and servers manage resources and services.

Each device has a specific role in maintaining stable communication across digital environments.

IP

IP Addressing

Every device in a network requires a unique IP address to communicate and exchange information.

TCP/IP

Protocols

Communication protocols define the rules used by devices to send and receive data.

LAN

Local Networks

Local Area Networks allow devices to connect efficiently inside homes, schools, and companies.

03

PACKET TRACER

Cisco Packet Tracer is a network simulation tool widely used in education and technical training.

It allows students to create virtual network infrastructures, configure routers and switches, test communication between devices, and simulate real networking environments without needing physical hardware.

Packet Tracer is essential for understanding how network systems operate in real-world scenarios.
